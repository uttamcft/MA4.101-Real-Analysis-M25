Elementary knowledge of Calculus. Much of mathematics relies on our ability to solve equations, whether explicitly or by establishing existence of solutions. Real Analysis provides a systematic and rigorous development of this material in the context of real-valued functions of a real variable.

Unit 1: Sequence of real numbers, Bounded and Unbounded Sets, Supremum, Infimum, Limit points, Closed Sets, Countable and uncountable sets, Convergence of sequences, Cauchy principle, Series convergence, Power series.

Unit 2: Mean value theorems, Taylor's Series, Partial derivatives, Integrals as limits, Fundamental theorem of Calculus, Multiple integrals.

Unit 3: Vectors, Gradients, Curl, Divergence, Differential equations, PDE elements.

Unit 4: Analytic functions, CR Equation, Laplace equation applications.

Unit 5: Complex function integration, Cauchy's theorem, Taylor & Laurent expansions, Residues, Contour integrals.

Project: To be submitted by semester end.

Assignments: 10 marks, Quizzes: 10 marks, Mid-Sem: 20 marks, End-Sem: 30 marks, Project: 30 marks.
