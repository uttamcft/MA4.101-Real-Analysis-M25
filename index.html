Prerequisite

Elementary knowledge of Calculus. Much of mathematics relies on solving equations. If not in explicit exact forms, then at least in establishing the existence of solutions. To do this requires a knowledge of so-called "analysis," which in many respects is just Calculus in very general settings. The foundations for this work are commenced in Real Analysis.
